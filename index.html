REL 372 Research Webpage

Research Details

In REL 372 "Gendered Authority in American Religion" we discussed many places over time that women have held limited religious authority in unique social spaces. As I scroll through my social medias, I can’t help but wonder if this is a modern space for Latter Day Saint women to contribute to religious authority in their social circles. This webpage is a summary of the research I performed for the final project in REL 327.

Questions

What religious or social benefits do social media accounts provide religious women? Is social media a space for these women to have a sense of religious authority?

Social Media Experiment

I wanted to know exactly how hard it was to create just three photos that were close to the quality of the photos I saw on my social media. After the help from three friends, $100 on new clothes, 2 hours of hair and makeup, one hour of taking photos, and three hours of editing I was able to produce three photos. Not surprisingly, this experiment was not easy. Through this process, I have gained a new respect for the women on the pages I see every day. It is a big commitment to post every day on social media and even bigger commitment to create crafted uniform content. This lead me to ask, why do some LDS women spend so much time crafting their online persona, and is it part of their personal religious expression?

Conclusion

Women of all religions have found religious authority in uncommon or "unofficial" spaces throughout time. To understand the religious history of America means to understand the religious history of women, and often that means looking at uncommon places. Will future research look to LDS social media in the hopes of gaining a well-rounded view of their religious history or will research still be inclined to analyze "official" sources of religion?
